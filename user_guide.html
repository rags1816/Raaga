1. Getting Started

Opening the App

Raaga runs entirely in your browser — no installation needed. Open it at rags1816.github.io/Raaga or by opening index.html from your Music folder.

1
See the welcome screen

The app opens with a startup screen showing instrument icons and version info.

2
Click "▶ Start Playing"

This is required — browsers block audio until the user interacts with the page first. The app won't make sound without this step.

3
You're in the Instruments tab

Press any key on your laptop keyboard or click a key on screen. You'll hear Harmonium by default.

💡 Use Chrome or Edge for best results. Safari has limited Web Audio support. Firefox works but may have slight audio delays.

Navigation

The tab bar at the top gives access to all sections. On smaller screens, tabs scroll horizontally. The AI Hub and ⚙ Admin buttons sit at the right end of the tab bar.

4. Practise Tab

The Practise tab is your daily training space. It combines free play with guided exercises and instant feedback.

Features

  • Tanpura Drone — continuous Sa drone to practise against. Turn on before starting any practice session.
  • Raaga Scale Display — shows which notes are correct (green) and which are forbidden (dimmed) for the selected raaga.
  • Note Feedback — each note you play lights up on the keyboard with its name shown in the status bar.
  • Raaga Quiz — tests your knowledge of raaga notes. +20 XP for correct answers.
  • Notation View — shows notes as Indian sargam notation as you play.

Good Practice Routine

1
Turn on Tanpura

Click "🪕 Tanpura Drone" — listen to the Sa and let your ear settle on it.

2
Select your raaga

Choose from the Raaga dropdown — the keyboard highlights valid notes in green.

3
Play the scale slowly

Go up: Sa Re Ga Ma Pa Dha Ni Sa' — then back down. Repeat until smooth.

4
Play alankars (patterns)

Try Sa Re Ga, Re Ga Ma, Ga Ma Pa... up and down the scale.

5
Improvise freely

Use only the highlighted green notes. Notice the mood of the raaga emerge.

5. Play-Along Tab

Karaoke-style learning — a backing melody plays and you play along on the keyboard. 65+ songs including Bollywood, devotional, classical, and folk.

How to use Play-Along

1
Select a song

Browse or search the song list. Songs show raaga, taal, and difficulty.

2
Choose instruments

Select backing instrument (what plays the melody for you to follow) and your instrument (what you play).

3
Set tempo

Start slower than normal — you can increase as you get comfortable.

4
Press Start

The highway shows notes coming — hit them as they reach the line. The backing rhythm and melody play automatically.

Play-Along Modes

ModeDescription
Listen ModeHear the full song 3 times before playing — absorb the melody first
Follow ModeBacking melody plays, you try to match it note for note
Free ModeRhythm plays, you improvise in the raaga
💡 Start with Listen Mode — Indian melodies are learned by ear first. Play-Along is designed to mimic the traditional guru-shishya approach where you hear before you play.

6. Raagas Reference Tab

A complete reference for all raagas in the app including scales, vadi/samvadi, time of day, season, and mood.

Understanding Raagas

A raaga is not just a scale — it is a complete musical personality with specific rules about which notes to use, which to emphasise, and how to move between them.

TermMeaning
ThaatParent scale the raaga belongs to (one of 10)
VadiThe most important note — the "king" of the raaga
SamvadiSecond most important note — the "minister"
AarohAscending scale — notes used going up
AvarohDescending scale — notes used going down (often different from aaroh)
PakadCharacteristic phrase that identifies the raaga
RasaEmotional quality — Shringar (love), Karuna (pathos), Shanta (peace) etc.

Open Admin Panel

Enable features

Toggle on the AI features you want to use.

Click AI Hub tab

Feature buttons appear. Click one to open its panel.

Keys are stored only in your browser's localStorage. They never appear in the app's source code. The app file (index.html) is completely safe to share publicly — it contains zero secrets. Keys are only ever sent directly to the AI provider (Anthropic or Google) when you use an AI feature.

⚠ If you clear your browser data or use a different browser/device, you'll need to re-enter your API keys.

13. Tips & Best Practices

For Beginners

  • Start with Yaman raaga — it uses Tivra Ma (F#) only, which makes it easy to identify. All other notes are natural.
  • Practice Sa Re Ga Ma Pa Dha Ni Sa slowly every day before anything else — this is your foundation.
  • Turn on the Tanpura drone every time you practice — training your ear to Sa is as important as knowing the notes.
  • Use Listen Mode in Play-Along before trying to play — Indian music is learned by ear first.
  • Start with Keherwa taal at 60–80 BPM. Add rhythm from the beginning — don't practice without it.

For Intermediate Players

  • Use the Raaga Tutor AI feature — it gives instant feedback if you accidentally use a forbidden note.
  • Explore the Gharana Analyser — describe your playing style and find out which tradition it resembles.
  • Try Bandish Composer — ask for a composition in your current raaga and learn it phrase by phrase.
  • Use Session Feedback after every practice — the AI tracks which notes you played most and gives targeted advice.

Using AI Effectively

  • The Guru Chat works best with specific questions — "What is the difference between Yaman and Kedar?" gives better answers than "tell me about raagas".
  • For Raaga Identifier, describe ornaments too — "S G M P with a meend from G to M" helps the AI distinguish between similar raagas.
  • Generate a Lesson Plan at the start of each week — tell it your current rank and what you want to focus on.

Performance Tips

  • Use Chrome for best audio performance — lowest latency, best WAV file support.
  • If audio feels delayed, reduce browser tabs — audio processing is CPU-intensive.
  • For mobile, use landscape orientation — more keyboard keys visible.
  • The app works offline once loaded (if opened from local file) — sounds are cached after first play.

Browser Console Errors (F12)

If something isn't working, press F12 → click Console tab. Red errors there will pinpoint exactly what's failing. Common ones:

  • 404 sounds/... — sounds folder not found relative to index.html
  • Failed to fetch — network issue or CORS block
  • AudioContext was not allowed — click Start Playing first
  • API key invalid — check key in Admin panel
