## Raaga grammar quick reference

### Yaman (Kalyan Thaat)
- **Scale:** S R G **m** P D N (uses Tivra Ma only)
- **Vadi:** G (Gandhar) · **Samvadi:** N (Nishad)
- **Characteristic phrase:** N R G m — G R S
- **Avoid:** Shuddh Ma (M)

### Bhairav (Bhairav Thaat)
- **Scale:** S **r** G M P **d** N
- **Vadi:** D (Dha) · **Samvadi:** r (Re)
- **Characteristic phrase:** S r G M — d N S
- **Avoid:** Shuddh Re, Shuddh Dha

### Bhairavi (Bhairavi Thaat)
- **Scale:** S **r** **g** M P **d** **n** (all komal except Sa, Ma, Pa)
- **Vadi:** M (Ma) · **Samvadi:** S (Sa)
- **Used in:** Thumri, closing of concerts
- **Note:** Very flexible — almost all notes permitted in practice

### Khamaj (Khamaj Thaat)
- **Scale:** S R G M P D **n** N (both Ni used)
- **Vadi:** G (Gandhar) · **Samvadi:** N (Nishad)
- **Note:** Komal Ni in descent, Shuddh Ni in ascent

### Bilawal (Bilawal Thaat)
- **Scale:** S R G M P D N (= Western major scale)
- **Vadi:** D (Dha) · **Samvadi:** G (Gandhar)
- **Morning raaga** — pure and bright

### Malkauns (Bhairavi Thaat)
- **Scale:** S **g** M **d** **n** (pentatonic — omits Re and Pa)
- **Vadi:** M (Ma) · **Samvadi:** S (Sa)
- **Midnight raaga** — deep and meditative

### Hamsadhwani (Bilawal Thaat)
- **Scale:** S R G P N (pentatonic — omits Ma and Dha)
- **Vadi:** G (Gandhar) · **Samvadi:** N (Nishad)
- **Auspicious** — often played at the start of concerts

### Shivaranjani (Kafi Thaat)
- **Scale:** S R **g** P D (pentatonic)
- **Characteristic:** No Ma or Ni — creates a very open, yearning feel
- **Popular** in South Indian film music
