Raaga Studio Pro — User Guide

Complete guide for students of Indian classical and world music · Works on desktop, tablet and mobile

Getting Started

1

Download & Open

Download raaga_studio_v4.html to your computer. Double-click it — it opens in your browser. No installation, no login, no internet needed after download. Works in Chrome, Edge, Safari and Firefox.

2

Click ▶ Start Playing

You must click this button first — browsers require a user action before any audio can play. The app loads instantly (no spinner, no CDN downloads). All sounds are generated live in your browser.

3

Turn on Tanpura Drone

Click the Tanpura Drone button in the Instrument section. This plays a continuous Sa drone (like a tambura/shruti box). Always keep it on while practising — it trains your ear to stay in tune with the root note.

4

Play your first note

Press A on your keyboard — you'll hear Sa (the root note, C4). Try the scale: ASDFGHJK = Sa Re Ga Ma Pa Dha Ni Sa'. On mobile: tap directly on the piano keys.

The 8 Tabs

🎹 Instruments 📖 Practise 🎮 Play-Along 🎼 Raagas 🎬 Songs 🎤 Sur & Shruti 🎓 Academy 📖 Theory

🎹 Instruments Tab

Your main playing screen. The top section has 6 collapsible panels — click any label (INSTRUMENT, TAAL/RHYTHM etc) to expand or collapse it. All start collapsed so the keyboard is prominent on screen.

  • Instrument — choose your sound. Start with Harmonium for Indian music.
  • Raaga Highlight — select a raaga to colour its notes green on the keyboard. Play only green keys to stay in the raaga.
  • Taal / Rhythm — select a rhythm cycle (try Keherva first). Press ▶ Play to start.
  • Tabla Octopad — 8 pads for tabla bols. Keys 1–8 on keyboard.
  • Tempo — drag slider or click Vilambit/Madhya/Drut presets.
  • Western Drum Kit — 9 drum pads, touch-friendly.
  • Piano Keyboard — 3 octaves. Taar (upper), Madhya (middle), Mandra (lower).
New feature: The upper octave (Taar Saptak) is now shown above the main keyboard — same size as other octaves, play it by touching or clicking.

📖 Practise Tab

Guided note-by-note learning with Guruji (AI music teacher). Load a song from the Songs tab first — then Guruji highlights each note in turn and corrects you immediately if you play wrong.

  • The current note glows orange on the keyboard — play it!
  • Correct note → Guruji praises you in Hindi ("Wah wah! Bilkul sahi!")
  • Wrong note → Guruji names the expected note and explains its role in the raaga
  • Auto-advance checkbox — automatically moves to next note when you're correct
  • ▶▶ Play Song — hear the whole melody automatically (demo mode)
  • Sargam Reference — horizontal table at the bottom showing all 12 notes
Best practice: Load a Beginner song, set Auto-advance ON, keep Tanpura on, and play each note as highlighted. Repeat the song 3–4 times before moving to Play-Along.

🎮 Play-Along Tab

Karaoke-style mode — the backing melody plays automatically while you match each note in time. Your accuracy is scored.

  • Load song inline — use the dropdown at the top of this tab (no need to go to Songs tab)
  • Speed — always start at 🐢 25% or 🐌 50%. Master slow before going faster.
  • Taal Backing — adds tabla rhythm. Keherva for Bollywood, Adi Talam for Tamil songs.
  • Hits/Misses/Accuracy — updated in real time as you play
  • Note strip — upcoming notes shown as pills: orange = current, teal = hit, rose = missed
  • Pause without losing score — press ⏸ and resume exactly where you stopped
BPM & Speed: BPM = Beats Per Minute — how fast the song plays. At 25% of 80 BPM you get 20 BPM — very slow, giving you plenty of time to find each note. Increase speed only when you can play at current speed with 80%+ accuracy.

🎼 Raagas Tab

Explore all 13 raagas used in Indian classical and film music.

  • Click any raaga card to see its full profile
  • Aaroh/Avaroh — ascending and descending scale (can differ!)
  • Vadi (green pill) — most important note, emphasise it often
  • Samvadi (rose pill) — second most important, consonant with vadi
  • ▶ Play Scale — hear the raaga's aaroh played on harmonium
  • Film songs listed — great for recognising raagas you already know
Start with Shivaranjani — only 5 notes (S R g P D). Select it in Raaga Highlight and play only green keys. You are playing a real raaga!

🎬 Songs Tab

Browse 47 songs across Bollywood (20), Tamil (14) and Western (13). Filter by language or difficulty. Each song card has three actions:

  • 🎵 Practise — guided mode with Guruji
  • ▶ Demo — auto-plays the melody so you can hear it
  • 🎮 Play-Along — goes directly to Play-Along mode

🎤 Sur & Shruti Tab

Find your natural Sa pitch and understand the fundamentals of pitch, rhythm and speed.

  • Shruti Grid — click each pitch button to hear it. Blue = male range (G3–B3), violet = both (C4–D4), rose = female range (E4–G4)
  • Sa Frequency — shows Hz of selected Sa and its Pa (fifth)
  • How to find your Sa: Hum "Aaa" comfortably, click pitches, the one where you feel most relaxed = your Sa
  • ▶ Sa + Pa — hear the fifth interval as a reference

Sur (Pitch), Laya (Rhythm), Taan (Speed Run), Taal (Meter) — theory blocks below the shruti grid explain each concept with examples.

🎓 Academy Tab

Track your progress and test your ear.

  • XP Bar — earns from correct notes (5 XP), completed songs (30 XP), Play-Along accuracy, and Raaga Quiz (20 XP)
  • Ranks: Novice Shishya → Shagird → Abhyasi → Tayyar → Ustadji → Sangeet Ratna
  • Level Roadmap — levels unlock as XP grows. Each level focuses on a different raaga.
  • ↺ Unlock to Revise — any locked level can be manually unlocked for revision without losing progress

🎯 Raaga Ear Quiz

Click ▶ Play Scale — a raaga scale plays on harmonium. Choose from 4 options which raaga it is. Correct = +20 XP + explanation. Wrong = shows the raaga's aaroh and vadi to help you learn.

Recommended Learning Path

Week 1 — First Steps

  • Turn on Tanpura. Press A–K to play the major scale (Sa Re Ga Ma Pa Dha Ni Sa')
  • Select Shivaranjani in Raaga Highlight. Play only green keys for 10 minutes.
  • Songs tab → Sa Re Ga Ma Scale → 🎵 Practise with Guruji
  • Try the Raaga Ear Quiz in Academy — even getting it wrong teaches you

Week 2 — First Song

  • Songs tab → Pehla Nasha (Beginner, Shivaranjani) → 🎵 Practise
  • Follow Guruji note by note. Repeat until you can play it from memory.
  • Play-Along tab → Pehla Nasha → 🐢 25% speed → ▶ Start
  • Aim for 60% accuracy before increasing to 50%

Week 3–4 — Add Rhythm

  • Instruments tab → Taal/Rhythm → Keherva (8 beats) → ▶ Play
  • Listen to Keherva for 2 minutes before touching the keyboard. Feel the beat cycle.
  • Play-Along with Keherva taal backing → Pehla Nasha at 50% speed
  • Learn second song: Tum Hi Ho (also Shivaranjani)

Month 2 — New Raaga

  • Raagas tab → click Yaman → note the Tivra Ma (F# — key T). That's what makes Yaman.
  • Songs → Ek Pyar Ka Nagma Hai (Yaman, Beginner) → practise
  • Tamil songs: Kadhal Rojave (Shivaranjani) → use Adi Talam backing
  • Academy quiz → try to identify Shivaranjani vs Yaman by ear

Month 3+ — Improvise

  • Pick any raaga → Raaga Highlight → play only green keys freely
  • Start on Sa, emphasise the Vadi note, end phrases on Sa — that's real raaga improvisation
  • Try upper octave (Taar Saptak) for ascending phrases — tap those smaller keys above the main keyboard
  • Western songs: Billie Jean uses Kafi raaga — same scale as Dorian mode in Western music

Tips & Troubleshooting

Common Questions

  • No sound? — Check the browser tab isn't muted (right-click tab → Unmute). Try Chrome if using another browser. Make sure you clicked ▶ Start Playing first.
  • Keyboard not responding? — Click anywhere on the page first (browser focus must be on the page, not another app).
  • What does Tanpura sound like? — A soft cycling drone: C3 → G3 → C4 → C4, repeating every 550ms. Like a tambura in the background.
  • Why can't I play the top octave with keyboard? — The Taar Saptak (upper octave) is touch/mouse only. The A–K keys play the middle octave; Z–M plays the lower octave.
  • My score resets when I pause — It shouldn't in this build. If it does, use ↺ Restart instead of Stop.
  • XP not saving? — The app uses browser localStorage. Make sure you're not in Incognito/Private mode.

Mobile & Touch Tips

  • All controls work by touch — keys, octopad, drum pads, sliders, collapsible headers
  • Landscape orientation recommended for the full keyboard
  • Scroll horizontally on the keyboard to reach all keys
  • Collapsible sections help save screen space on phones — collapse what you don't need
  • On iOS Safari: works in Safari only (Chrome on iOS uses WebKit but may have Web Audio quirks)
  • On Android: Chrome recommended
  • Pinch-to-zoom is allowed — use it to zoom in on the keyboard if needed
