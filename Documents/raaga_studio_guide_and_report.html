Suggested Improvements

Fix pause/resume in Play-Along HIGH

When you pause and resume a song, the score resets to zero because resumePlayAlong() calls startPlayAlong() again from the top. The fix is to track elapsed time properly.

// CURRENT (broken): function resumePlayAlong(){ paActive=true; paPaused=false; paStart=Date.now()-paCurrentIdx*paMsPerBeat; startPlayAlong(); // ← this resets everything! } // FIX: restart the interval from current position only function resumePlayAlong(){ paActive=true; paPaused=false; paStart=Date.now()-paCurrentIdx*paMsPerBeat; Tone.Transport.start(); document.getElementById('pa-btn-play').disabled=true; document.getElementById('pa-status-txt').textContent='▶ Resumed!'; paTimer=setInterval(paTickFn, 50); // reuse existing tick }

Save XP & progress to localStorage HIGH

All practice XP and Academy level progress is lost when you close the browser. A simple localStorage save would make learning feel progressive and motivating.

// Add to awardXP() after updating totalXP: localStorage.setItem('raagaStudioXP', totalXP); // Add to app startup (after buildAllKeys etc.): const savedXP = parseInt(localStorage.getItem('raagaStudioXP')) || 0; if(savedXP > 0) awardXP(savedXP, null);

Fix setSpeed() event.target reference MEDIUM

The speed buttons pass no argument to setSpeed(), relying on an implicit global event. This is fragile — pass the element directly instead.

// HTML: change onclick to pass 'this' onclick="setSpeed(0.5, this)" // JS: accept el as second parameter function setSpeed(mult, el){ paSpeedMult=mult; document.querySelectorAll('.speed-btn').forEach(b=>b.classList.remove('active')); if(el) el.classList.add('active'); }

Add more Tamil film songs MEDIUM

The app has great Bollywood coverage but only 1–2 Tamil songs (Kadhal Rojave, Malare Mounama). Adding Ilaiyaraaja and AR Rahman Tamil classics would make it more complete for Tamil learners. Suggestions: Poovizhi Vasalile (Kaamraj), Poo Malai Vaangi (Sindhubhairavi), Uyire Uyire (Bombay), Kannalanae (Bombay).

Show notation alongside keyboard highlighting MEDIUM

When a raaga is highlighted on the keyboard, also show a small aaroh/avaroh strip above the keys. This reinforces the connection between the visual keys and written notation simultaneously.

Allow pinch-zoom on mobile LOW

Remove user-scalable=no from the viewport meta tag. The keyboard already has horizontal scroll, so zoom is not harmful and helps accessibility.

<meta name="viewport" content="width=device-width,initial-scale=1.0">

Add a "slow practice" speed to Play-Along LOW

Currently speeds are 0.5×, 0.75×, 1×. Adding a 0.25× "super slow" option would help beginners in the first few days of learning a song.

Notation Tab — Guided Practice

Learn songs note by note with the guided notation trainer

  • 1First go to the Songs tab and click Practice on any song. This loads it into the Notation trainer.
  • 2The current note to play is highlighted in pink on the mini keyboard. Match it on your computer keyboard or click it.
  • 3Press ▶ Next Note to step through manually, or ▶▶ Play Song to auto-advance at the song's tempo.
  • 4The sargam notation scrolls across the top — uppercase = natural (shuddh), lowercase = flat (komal), m = tivra (sharp Ma only).
  • 5Use Show key hints to see which keyboard letter matches each note. Uncheck it once you start memorising.
The sargam row: S = Sa · r = Komal Re · R = Re · g = Komal Ga · G = Ga · M = Ma · m = Tivra Ma · P = Pa · d = Komal Dha · D = Dha · n = Komal Ni · N = Ni · Ṡ = Upper Sa
🎮

Play-Along Tab — Karaoke Mode

Test your speed and accuracy — hit notes as they fall

  • 1Select a song from the Songs tab and click the 🎮 button — or click 🎮 Play-Along from the Notation tab.
  • 2The note strip at the top shows upcoming notes. The current note glows orange and scales up.
  • 3Set speed to 0.5× while learning — the notes come at half the original tempo.
  • 4Choose a taal (rhythm cycle) — Keherwa (8 beats) is the most common in Bollywood songs. This adds tabla accompaniment while you play.
  • 5After the song ends, your accuracy % is shown and XP is awarded. Aim for 80%+ before moving to the next song.
The highway canvas shows notes scrolling down — pink = current target, teal = hit, red = missed.
🎼

Raagas Tab — Learn the Scales

Explore 12 raagas used in Bollywood and Tamil cinema

  • 1Click any raaga card to open its full profile — notes, aaroh (ascending), avaroh (descending), vadi, samvadi, and famous songs.
  • 2The vadi (green pill) is the most important note — emphasise it when improvising. The samvadi (red pill) is the second most important.
  • 3Click 🎹 Show on keyboard to highlight that raaga's notes on the Keyboard tab and hear how the scale sounds.
  • 4Click any 🎮 song button in the raaga panel to jump directly into Play-Along for that song.
🎬

Songs Tab — Browse & Play

34 songs — Bollywood, Tamil, Michael Jackson, ABBA, Boney M

Shruti Box Tab — Tune Your Ear

A continuous drone reference for ear training

  • 1Select any note from the grid — this sets your Sa (root). Default is C4.
  • 2Click ▶ Play Sa Reference to hear a sustained tanpura-style drone.
  • 3Sing or hum along to train your ear to recognise Sa. Then try humming Re, Ga, etc. above it.

Recommended Learning Path

1

Week 1 — Read the notation, play Sa Re Ga Ma

Go to Notation tab reference. Memorise: A=Sa, S=Re, D=Ga, F=Ma, G=Pa, H=Dha, J=Ni, K=upper Sa. Play the Bilawal Scale exercise song up and down.

2

Week 2 — Shivaranjani pentatonic (5 notes only)

Highlight Shivaranjani on the keyboard. The 5 notes are S R g P D. Practise Pehla Nasha and Tum Hi Ho in Notation mode at 0.5× speed.

3

Week 3 — Play-Along your first song

Load Pehla Nasha in Play-Along at 0.5×. Aim for 60% accuracy first time, then 80%+ after repeating. Collect XP to unlock Academy levels.

4

Week 4 — Yaman (7 notes, tivra Ma)

The characteristic note of Yaman is tivra Ma (key T). Learn Ek Pyar Ka Nagma Hai and Ajeeb Dastan Hai Yeh. Read the Raagas tab for its aaroh/avaroh.

5

Ongoing — Explore at full speed + try Tamil songs

Once you've done Shivaranjani and Yaman, explore Bhairavi and Khamaj. Try Kadhal Rojave (Shivaranjani) and the Malkauns songs. Use the Shruti box to practise singing.
